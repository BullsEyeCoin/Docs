Bulls Eye is not fake-renounced theater.

The owner keeps the knobs needed to keep the game alive, but the important limits are public.

## Owner controls

The owner can:

- Adjust buy tax and sell tax up to the hard cap
- Adjust the minimum qualifying buy
- Adjust jackpot odds settings
- Update router, swap, and randomness configuration
- Rescue unrelated ERC-20 tokens accidentally sent to the contracts

## Vault safeguards

The live jackpot is not meant to be an always-open wallet.

- Emergency native withdrawal requires shutdown first
- Emergency native withdrawal also requires a delay before funds can move
- That is there to reduce the chance of the jackpot looking like a free sweep button

## Public trust model

The project still has active owner controls because launch conditions can change fast.

Those controls exist so the team can react if:

- jackpots are hitting too often
- jackpots are too hard to hit
- the minimum buy needs to move
- taxes need to be lowered
- odds need to be rebalanced

## Verification

The important part is this: buys, draws, payouts, and jackpot state should all be traceable on-chain.
